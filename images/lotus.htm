PROJECTS

VOICE & AUDIO INTERACTION

Tour the online world with ears. Technology use has long been relying on visual access. Progress in voice technology now allows us to utilize our hearing in navigating online content, hands-free and eyes-free. Yet, most websites are not readily designed for voice interaction. We conduct a series of interview studies to explore this design challenge - how should different forms of online content be “translated” into an audio format?

Advisor: Leah Findlater, University of Washington, HCDE

KEYWORDS:

Human Robot Interaction, Affective Computing, Behavior Engineering

INPUT ACCESSIBILITY

Re-evaluate input performance at varying age and motor ability. In this study, we critically examined the nuanced relationships between age, motor ability, and input performance through analyzing a dataset from a large-scale study that captures input performance with a mouse and/or touchscreen from over 700 participants. Our analysis illustrates the continuous relationship between age, motor ability, and input performance, but also suggests that knowing a user’s age and self-reported motor ability should not lead to assumptions about their input performance.

Advisor: Leah Findlater, University of Washington, HCDE

KEYWORDS:

Human Robot Interaction, Affective Computing, Behavior Engineering

ROBOT ACCESSIBILITY

Survey accessibility guidelines among social robot designers. Standards and guidelines are powerful tools to legislate and regulate the design of products, services and infrastructures to make them accessible. Yet, it can be challenging to come up with applicable, flexible, and informing guidelines for a specific area, such as robotics. We conduct surveys and literature review among social robot scholars and engineers to understand how they perceive and practice accessible design on their robots.

Advisor: Elin Bjorling, University of Washington, HCDE

KEYWORDS:

Human Robot Interaction, Affective Computing, Behavior Engineering

MIXED REALITY USABILITY

Facilitate Mixed reality usability analysis. It is challenging to perform low-cost usability testing with Virtual, Augmented reality tools. In this project, I supported the development and evaluation of MRAT, an analysis tool for assisting the usability and user experience testing of Mixed Reality applications. My contributions include tool development, user study setup, and competitive analysis.

Advisor: Michael Nebeling, University of Michigan, iSchool

KEYWORDS:

Human Robot Interaction, Affective Computing, Behavior Engineering

CUDDLEBIT

Calm your emotion with the furry robots. How does the sense of touch communicate emotion? We explored how people perceive and empathize with different robot movements with a set of user studies and short interviews. We learned that under certain narratives, it is possible for us to empathize with automatically generated robot emotions, but controlling the narratives is a major challenge for affective computing.

Advisor and mentor: Karon MacLeon, Paul Bucci, University of British Columbia, CS

KEYWORDS:

Human Robot Interaction, Affective Computing, Behavior Engineering

DOUGHNUT

Motivate online donation with community involvement and recognition. A small A/B testing project for exploring the effects of emphasizing community involvement and recognition in designing an online donation website.

KEYWORDS:

Field Study, Web UX Design, Strategic Prototyping, Usability Testing
